# Commercial Rights Options (Negotiation Menu)

Use this document during commercial discussions to keep rights decisions explicit.

Default state:
- All IP rights are retained by Licensor.
- No commercial rights are granted unless signed.

## Option 1: Internal Fork Only (Royalty-Free)

Typical use:
- internal operations only
- one legal entity
- no external commercialization

Ownership:
- retained by Licensor
- derivatives retained by Licensor unless explicitly granted otherwise

## Option 2: Commercial License Grant

Typical use:
- customer delivery, productization, hosted offering, or revenue use

Negotiation variables:
- field of use
- territory
- term
- exclusivity/non-exclusivity
- sublicensing/assignment rights
- support/maintenance obligations
- royalty or fee model

Ownership:
- retained by Licensor unless separately transferred

## Option 3: Full IP Assignment / Ownership Transfer

Typical use:
- client wants full ownership and control
- IP will be core enterprise asset

Terms to define:
- assigned assets and commit scope
- derivative/improvement ownership treatment
- moral rights waiver/consent where applicable
- trademark/branding carve-outs
- transition/support obligations
- payment model (paid, hybrid, or royalty-free only if Licensor explicitly approves in writing)

## Commercial Trigger Statement (Reusable)

"Any commercialization, productization, revenue use, or use of this IP as a core enterprise asset requires a separate signed commercial rights agreement. Default ownership and derivative rights remain with Licensor unless explicitly transferred in writing."
